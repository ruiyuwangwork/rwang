Overview¶

I am now a PhD student in Temple University. My supervisors are Dr. Eric Borguet and Dr. Vincenzo Carnevale. I am studying water behavior in several enviroments, especially water/alumina interfaces. In the past, I spent a lot of work on chemical reactions in aqueous solutions, ion adsorption at water/alumina interfaces using both classical and ab initio molecular dynamics simulations, especially free energy calculations using enhanced sampling techniques.

Currently, I am investigating the pattern of amino acid adsorption and the topology of interfacial hydrogen bonds. Besides physical chemistry, I am also interested in modern technologies such as machine learning and its applications, especially, how these tools and proper physics models are used in big data and business analysis.

I am about to graduate at the end of this year and I am open to job market.
